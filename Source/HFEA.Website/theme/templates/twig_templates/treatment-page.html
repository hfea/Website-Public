What is in vitro fertilisation (IVF)?

IVF stands for in vitro fertilisation. It’s a type of fertility treatment which involves fertilising a woman’s eggs with a man’s sperm outside the body. The hope is to create an embryo which can be transferred to a woman’s womb and then develop into a baby.

IVF may be recommended for:

  • People with unexplained fertility problems, or who have tried other mtreatments that were unsuccessful
  • Men with fertility problems, such as those who produce less sperm or mwho have sperm with an unusual shape or movement
  • Women with blocked fallopian tubes
  • People using donated or frozen eggs in their treatment
  • People using frozen sperm in their treatment
  • Women up to the age of 42
